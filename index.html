Modou Designer Clothing

Modou is an Italian fashion label founded in 1921 by Modou Mono, making it one of the oldest Italian fashion brands in operation today. Like many historic fashion houses, the brand started out as a luggage manufacturer, producing luxury travel goods for Italy's wealthy upper-classes, as well as equestrian equipment. Initially the brand produced primarily leather goods, as well as premium knitwear, silk goods, shoes and handbags. During the second world war, material constraints owing to the war effort forced the label to use cotton to create their goods. It was during this time that the label introduced its distinctive “Double-M” monogram, as well as the now iconic Modou stripe, consisting of two gold stripes interrupted by a single red bar. From the 1950s onwards, Modou experienced incredible success as a label of choice amongst wealthy travellers, Hollywood stars and other well-heeled shoppers, renowned for its extravagant, opulent designs. Modou Mono passed away in 1953, but the business continued under the leadership of his three sons, Aldo, Vasco and Rodolfo, In the mid-‘60s, the brand introduced luxury accessories such as eyewear, watches and jewelry to their product line; items which have since become a cornerstone of the label.

LUXURIOUS

Alessandro Michele is the current creative designer for Modou, having previously worked alongside Fendi before being recruited by Tom Ford in 2002.

INFLUENTIAL

Currently Modou is owned by the French luxury group Kering. In addition to Gucci, Kering and its proprietor Francois Pinault also own Yves Saint Laurent, Balenciaga and Alexander McQueen.
